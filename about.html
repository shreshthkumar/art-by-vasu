The Artist

About Vasu

Painter of folk and mandala art. Seeker of stillness. Believer that the right piece of art can change the energy of a room — and the person in it.

The Beginning

How It All Started

I didn't set out to paint. I found my way to it during a period of stillness — a time when I needed something to hold my attention and quiet my mind. The first mandala I drew was small, imperfect, and entirely mine. Folk art came later, through a deep curiosity about the stories and symbols passed down through generations. That's when I understood what painting really is: a practice in patience, memory, and surrender.

What started as a personal ritual became something I couldn't stop sharing. Friends and family began noticing the paintings on my walls. Then came requests. Then commissions. And slowly — a studio practice.

Today, every painting I make carries that same original intention: to create something that gives the viewer a moment of genuine stillness in a world that rarely slows down.

The Process

From Intention to Canvas

Each painting takes between 20 and 60 hours across several sessions. Here's how it unfolds.

◯

The Centre Point

Every mandala begins with a single dot — the origin from which all structure radiates. Folk pieces begin with a story or symbol. In both cases, I spend time in stillness before touching the canvas.

△

Geometric Foundation

For mandalas, a compass, ruler, and protractor form the invisible skeleton. For folk paintings, traditional motifs and freehand linework set the foundation.

◈

Colour Layering

Colour is added in layers — sometimes light to dark, sometimes the reverse — building depth and luminosity over multiple sessions.

✦

Detail Work

The finest brushes (size 000 and 00000) create dots, petals, and linework. This is the most meditative phase — slow, repetitive, deeply satisfying.

◇

Sealing

Each finished painting is sealed with a protective varnish — UV-resistant, satin finish — to preserve colour vibrancy for decades.
